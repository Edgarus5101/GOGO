Value Chain

1. Key Actors

  • -Producers of Chili and Spices
  • -Input Suppliers
  • -Transformers and Processors
  • -Packagers
  • -Distributors and Exporters
  • -Governmental and Regulatory Institutions
  • -End Consumers

2. Preproduction

  • Crop Planning: Determining required land, selecting chili and spice varieties, and managing resources.
  • Policies and Regulations: Compliance with phytosanitary standards, export permits, and certifications.
  • Agricultural Inputs: Procuring high-quality seeds, fertilizers, and tools for cultivation.

3. Production

  • Yahualica Chili Cultivation: Planting, maintaining, and harvesting at peak ripeness.
  • Other Spice Cultivation: Growing cumin, oregano, pepper, and more.
  • Quality Control: Ensuring export-quality products (pest-free, optimal size, good flavor).

4. Postproduction Operations

  • Drying and Processing: Properly drying and processing chili and spices.
  • Transformation and Value Addition: Creating sauces, spice blends, or chili powder.
  • Packaging: Preserving product quality with market-ready packaging.

5. Marketing and Distribution

  • Marketing: Promoting Yahualica Chili as a premium product in international markets.
  • Distribution: Managing logistics, customs compliance, and delivery to retailers or consumers.
  • Sales: Selling in wholesale markets, specialty stores, or e-commerce platforms.
